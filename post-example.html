Devlog: building Neon Rush in a weekend

PublishedJune 14, 2026
AuthorBloxdStudios Team
Read time6 min

We built and scrapped three versions of this map before the one that's live now. This is a short account of what went wrong the first two times, and what we changed on the third try. Replace this text with your own devlog.

Attempt one: too many shortcuts

Our first layout had five branching paths, and playtesters just got lost. A parkour map lives or dies on whether a new player can read the route at a glance, and we'd buried the main path under our own cleverness.

A good shortcut should feel like a secret you found, not a fork you got lost in.

Attempt two: too safe

We overcorrected and stripped it down to one straight line. It was easy to read and instantly boring — nobody replayed it twice. The map needed some risk back in it.

What finally worked

  • One clear main path, with shortcuts branching visibly off it instead of replacing it.
  • Checkpoints placed so a failed shortcut never costs more than a few seconds.
  • We play-tested with three friends, not just ourselves — self-testing hides how hard a jump actually is.

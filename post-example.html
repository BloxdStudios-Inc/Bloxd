Why we rebuilt our design system from scratch

PublishedJune 14, 2026
AuthorStudio Team
Read time6 min

We've rebuilt our design system three times in four years. This is a short account of what went wrong the first two times, and what we changed on the third try. Replace this text with your own post.

Attempt one: too much, too soon

Our first system tried to solve every problem at once — theming, animation, accessibility variants — before we had more than a handful of real screens to test it against. It collapsed under its own scope within a quarter.

A design system should describe the product you have, not the one you're planning to build in two years.

Attempt two: too rigid

The second version overcorrected. Every component had exactly one allowed variant, which was great for consistency and terrible for actual product work. Designers routed around it instead of using it.

What finally worked

  • We started from real screens already in production, not a blank Figma file.
  • Tokens came before components — color, spacing and type first.
  • One person owned the system part-time, instead of it being everyone's side project.
